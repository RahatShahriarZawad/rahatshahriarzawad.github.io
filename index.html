Bio

I am an MS student in Computer Science at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa. As a Graduate Research Assistant at the Hawaii Digital Health Lab, under the mentorship of Dr. Peter Washington, my work centers on building data-driven, AI-enabled solutions that address real-world health challenges — with a strong emphasis on inclusivity and ethical practice. My responsibilities span data acquisition and analysis, algorithm development, and designing interventions for early detection and accessible healthcare.

I hold a BSc in Information and Communication Engineering from Bangladesh University of Professionals, where I conducted research under Dr. M. Shamim Kaiser. My undergraduate work in predictive modeling and data-driven methods laid the foundation for my current focus on fair, equitable AI technologies that serve societal needs. I am motivated by the intersection of research and teaching, aspiring to shape healthcare technology through an ethical and rigorous lens.
