Bio

Md Rahat Shahriar Zawad

MS Student (Computer Science)

Department of ICS, University of Hawaii at Manoa, Hawaii, USA

I am an MS student in Computer Science at the University of Hawaii at Manoa. My work as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Hawaii Digital Health Lab, under the mentorship of Dr. Peter Washington, involves building data-driven, AI-enabled solutions that address real-world health challenges with a strong emphasis on inclusivity and ethical practices. This role encompasses everything from acquiring and analyzing complex health data to developing algorithms that support early detection and accessible healthcare interventions. Additionally, as a Teaching Assistant, I guide students in mastering database management and foundational computer science, fostering both my own growth and that of future tech innovators.

I hold a BSc in Information and Communication Engineering from the Bangladesh University of Professionals, where I conducted research under the guidance of Dr. M. Shamim Kaiser. My undergraduate work in predictive modeling and data-driven approaches laid the groundwork for my current focus on ethical AI technologies that serve societal needs. Committed to advancing digital health technologies that are fair, equitable, and impactful, I am motivated by a mission to merge research and teaching, aspiring to shape the future of healthcare technology with a strong ethical foundation.
